General

  • Maintain physical and mental health

    • Know where to seek health in case of a breakdown
    • Eat well and exercise as needed

  • Advisor-advicee relationship should be professional

    • Otherwise it can get difficult to work efficiently
    • Both parties need to be able to 'not-care' too much about each others' personal matters

  • Be confident.

    • Being assertive is often not a bad thing
    • Set boundaries.

  • Take breaks. Sometimes breakthroughs come when you are not fixating too much about it.

  • Advisor advises. Decision is up to you.

    • Advisor is not a commander
    • Student should be in charge of their PhD
    • Student should make well informed decision with the help of advisor

  • PhD => you'll be on your own most of the time

  • Don't underappreciate significance of paperwork like reports. They are often very helpful.

  • Quals: By the time it's time for your quals, your state should be such that the quals are 'trivial to pass'หก.

  • Funding: Search for fellowships, TA-ships, grants, etc

Finding an advisor

  • A good advisor is more central to your PhD than the university
  • Personality of advisor should be on that you are comforable with
    • 'many people underestimate the importance of personality match.' หก
  • How's the lab environment? Work culture?
    • Are lab mates getting along? Or are they competing with each other?
    • Regular research discussions/talks
    • Visits from other institutes
    • Tie-ups with industry
    • How many other potential advisors in same institute?
  • What happens if advisor resigns or takes sabbatical?

Advisor

  • Introduce advicee to others in the research community
  • Regularly meet advicee
  • Conduct group meetings for discussions
    • This is in addition to one-to-one meets with advicees.
  • Help advicee procure funding
  • Help advicee develop skills to become an independent researcher
    • Literature review skills
    • Writing skills
    • Presentation skills
  • Let advicee know about confs/events that can be helpful for their academic growth
  • Ideal advisor: Not too strict nor too loose is the best way
    • Lets the student explore
    • Rein in the student if they trail too far from the right path
  • Judge viability of advicee's outlandish ideas
    • Help advicee manage their expectations
  • Doesn't brand advicee questions stupid
    • If the questions is irrelevent, help the student understand why it is so
    • Help advicee to think systematically and come up with relevant questions
    • One of the core aims of a Phd is to produce a independent researcher
  • Direct communication is best
    • Don't leave the advicee to guess what you meant/implied.

Advicee

  • Regulary meet advisor
    • Update progress, impediments, thoughts
    • Eg: Weekly
  • Learn how to
    • ask the right questions
    • organize your thoughts
    • critically analyse idea/papers
  • Meeting with advisor
    • Take notes
    • Have action items for next meet
    • Send a minutes of your meet to advisor on same day
    • Advisor can clear up mistakes/confusion after seeing the minutes
  • Advisors usually have much more in their hands than students
  • Don't expect advisor to remember what was said in the previous meet
    • "advisors are stateless" - Mae Milano
  • Communications should be kept short and precise.
    • Reduces time wastage.
  • Be honest with the advisor
    • If an advicee can't be honest with their advisor, that relationship has no chance of working out
  • Figure out how best to work together
    • You will need to align yourself with your advisor's preferred style of working
  • Advisor won't have much time for small things
    • Ask fellow students and Postdocs before going to advisor
    • Mentoring by senior stduents
    • This is something that you would also do once senior enough
  • Plan your meets with advisor
    • Have precise points to discuss
    • If there was no progress, just say so (as long as it doesn't happen too much!)
    • Meet advisor even if you feel there is no progress to report.
  • Respect advisor's time. Schedule meets in advance.
  • Resist temptation to make up stuff to cover for work you didn't do
    • It will hurt you and waste advisor's time
    • Be honest. Direct communication is best in formal academic life.

Time management

  • Being a good researcher is more about being consistent than being smart.
  • Effective time management is vital for this.
  • Have a calendar with your activities charted out.
    • https://deviparikh.medium.com/calendar-in-stead-of-to-do-lists-9ada86a512dd
  • Break down big tasks into small managable chunks
    • SMART: Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound

Writing

  • Have a writing plan
  • Initial draft: Write whatever that comes to mind
  • You will need numerous iterations/revisions
  • Be patient, don't let yourself go mad

Be mindful of future

Thesis usually doesn't matter

  • Academica: Papers published
  • Industry: Degree

Do what interests you. your passion matters. Work shouldn't feel like a chore. This is ideal PhD.
