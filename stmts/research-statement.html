Research statement

What's the motivation?

  • Why are you doing this project?
  • What need does it solve?
  • What would change if this is implemented?
  • Who will benefit?

How do you intend to showcase your results?

Have a few example scenarios and work toward a solution to those problems. Results can be showcased as solutions to that problem

Focus on result not method

When I was working on my project, I often got lost learning how to use tools instead of figuring out how to use those tools for my problem. Like learning tikz and metapost, when I had a presentation to make the next day.

If you aim is to draw a picture, your focus should be on making that picture, not on enusring that the pencil looks cool. Don't miss the forest for the trees.

Tools are not important. Concepts are. It's the ideas that matter.

Attitude not current knowledge

Often it's not the amount of knowledge that you posses but your attitude that matters.
