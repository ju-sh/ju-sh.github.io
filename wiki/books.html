Truth and lies

I agree with the author. One cannot lie without knowing that he is lying. Because for one to lie, he must be saying something that goes against what he believes to be the truth. And what he believes as the truth may not be the truth.

And as a side note, I guess it is possible that what one person considers as the truth may not be what another person considers to be the truth. What the first person considers to be the truth may even be something that another person considers to be a lie. There may be dependency on a definition of truth itself that may change based on perspective.

Godelian puzzle book - Smullyan

  • Envelope1: The sentences on the two envelopes are both false
  • Envelope2: The dime is in the other envelope.

Solution:

Consider sentence on envelope1.

Two cases.

If sentence on envolope1 was true, then both sentences are false. But that includes sentence1 as well. => Contradiction.

So sentence1 can't true. It must be false (assuming law of excluded middle, I guess). That means at least one of the two sentences is true.

So, sentence2 is true.

∴ the coin is in the 1st envolope.

  • [m(a) ∧ e(b)] ∨ [e(a) ∧ m(b)]

  • Envolope1: ¬a ∨ ¬b

  • Envolope2: m(b)

  • m(b) is false
  • contradiction since we know one envolope has money

If a ∧ b:

  • m(b) is true. ie, S2 is true

So money is in envolope2.
