Effects

  • Leidenfrost effect:
    • A very hot solid surface immersed in a liquid
    • Temperature of solid surface >> Temperature of liquid's boiling point
    • Leidenfrost point
    • Liquid droplets hover over the surface rather than making contact
    • Because the liquid vapourises, producing an vapour layer over the solid
    • This prevents the remaining liquid from boiling rapidly
    • Example: Water on hot cooking pan. Balls of water seem to hover.
