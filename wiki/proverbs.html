Proverbs

From all over the world. :-)

Some may not really be proverbs. Corrections appreciated.

Getting things done

  • He who asks a question is a fool for five minutes; he who doesn’t ask a question is a fool for ever. (Chinese)
  • Slow and steady wins the race.
    • മെല്ലെ തിന്നാൽ പനയും തിന്നാം.
  • Rome was not built in a day.
  • Those who wish to sing, always finds a song.
  • The best time to plant a tree was twenty years ago; the second best time is now.
  • Journey of a thousand miles begins with a single step ¹
    • Chinese: 千里之行，始於足下
    • Pinyin: Qiānlǐ zhī xíng, shǐyú zú xià.
    • Literal meaning: ‘A journey of a thousand li starts beneath one’s feet.’
  • We cannot direct the wind, but we can adjust the sails. (German proverb?) ⁷
  • A rolling stone gathers no moss.
    • An idle mind is devil’s workshop.
  • Too many cooks spoil the broth.

  • A faithful friend is a sure shelter, whoever finds one has found a rare treasure.
