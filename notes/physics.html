Hydrostatic pressure

Pressure exerted by a column of stationary liquid on the walls of its container.

SI unit: N.m⁻²

P = ρgh

where:

  • ρ: density of liquid
  • g: acceleration due to gravity
  • h: height of the liquid column

Relation between force and pressure

F = P.A

where:

  • P: Pressure
  • A: Area

SI unit of pressure: N.m⁻² = Pa (ie, Pascal)

When we are calculating force exerted on a surface due to pressure, we consider only a part of that surface whose area is A.

Mass flow rate

Rate with respect to time at which mass going out of a 'container':

r = ρ * A * v

where:

  • ρ: density of liquid
  • A: Area of cross-section of pipe
  • v: velocity of fluid

Longitudinal vs transverse waves

  • Longitudinal: Direction of propagation is same as the direction in which variation/vibration is occurring.
    • aka pressure waves
  • Transverse: Direction of propagation is perpendicular to the direction in which variation is occurring.

Eg:

  • Sound waves in air are longitudinal.
    • Compression/decompression of air
