Latin words

Corrections appreciated!

  • Scribitur ad narrandum, non ad probandum: History is written to narrate, not to prove
  • Nuntii Latini: Latin news ¹
  • Ut est rerum omnium magister usus: Experience is the teacher of all things ²
  • quod erat demonstrandum: which was to be demonstrated ³
  • lapsus linguae: slip of the tongue
  • nota benea: note well
  • Quod non: which is not true ⁴
  • simplex sigillum veri: simplicity is the seal of truth
  • materia medica: medical material
  • a priori: before-hand
  • posteriori: what one knows afterwards (eg: after a math proof is done)
  • Non est regia ad Geometriam via.: There is no royal road to geometry. ⁵
  • fons et origo: source and origin
  • ex falso [sequitur] quodlibet: from falsehood, anything [follows] ⁶
  • ex contradictione [sequitur] quodlibet: from contradiction, anything [follows] ⁶
  • respice finem: look at the end
  • novare: to make new ⁷
  • dum: while
  • nox: night / darkness
  • nocte: night
  • capit: captures
  • dolore: pain
  • anima: soul
  • corpus: body
  • vita: life
  • canticum: song
  • mea: my / mine
    • mea culpa: my fault
  • miranda: to be wondered at ʳ
  • pro bono publico (often shortened to 'pro bono'): for the public good.
