Ta's timeless song

I was a bit annoyed that day. There was someone going around the office asking what they would do if they had a time machine and could go back in time. It sounded like a good way to waste other people's time if anything. And I had work to do.

I left office

I entered my room and opened my wardrobe to put away my things. Inside of the wardrobe was a mess as usual. But I felt a sudden urge to clean up. Although I knew full well that it would revert to its normal state within a couple of days.

As I was rummaging about one of the shelves, I found something that was once very dear to me, but had since been neglectedâ€¦ My diary. I used to write in it almost every day for years. I had filled many diaries. This was the last one.

I smiled. A smile of sadness maybe? A feeling of guilt that I hadn't written anything in a long time? Or a smile of happiness? Because I knew that I was holding a record of my past in my hands?

Either way, I opened it. I saw things that made me smile more. And some things that made me sad. Then I saw something that had me hooked. It was my 20 year old self mentioning something that the 15-year old me had seen.

That made me smile broadly. Both outwards and in my mind.

Then I realized that I knew which point in my life would I relive for a moment if I could.

It was to listen to Ta singing her song again.

Back when I was in 10th grade, after multiple prior attempts, my family finally succeeded in carting me off to a hostel away from home so that they didn't have to deal with me. They paid the hostel warden to deal with me instead. I resented my parents for that.

We could go home once a month. I usually had no wish to go home. Why hurry to meet those who didn't wish to have you around? But there was something that attracted me back home: Computer games.

My siblings were all away at college. I could have the computer all to myself. And at that age, I played video games whenever I could. That was the only reason I went home for the holidays.

Staying back at hostel wasn't too bad actually. Because during the holidays, most students would go home and only very few would remain. Because of our small number, the wardens didn't bother us at all. We could do whatever we wanted. There was no timetable to follow except for food.

But the computer at home was always a way bigger attractive force. So I went home almost every month. And as soon as reaching there, I would switch on the computer. My family tolerated this. They understood full well why I came home.

Once I went home as usual. I saw that my aunt and her two girls, my little cousins, were there. Well, they are not little anymore, but they were at the time. I liked to call them Na and Ta.

Na was elder, an intelligent and caring girl of around 10. Ta, the younger, was not at all reserved and would say whatever that would come to her mind. She was only around 4 at the time. Na used to be the opposite of being reserved when she was of Ta's age, but she talked less and listened more as she got older. Like a good big sister, she was protective of Ta.

Na and Ta had come for a visit with their mother, my aunt, and were staying the night. They hadn't known that I would be home that day. But the kids were happy to see me. I was happy to see them too. They were favourite cousins. But then I remembered that I couldn't be at my computer without ignoring them. And I liked them enough to hate to ignore them.

Na, being perceptive, realized that something was bothering me.. though she didn't talk to me about it. But Ta saw nothing of that and was very happy to be near my mother and me with her family. We talked. I, sometimes absent-mindedly with my eyes on the computer. Sometime later, Ta was too excited to sit down. She began hopping all around us. Half-dancing, half running. Finally she began singing. A song she learned from her day care centre. I was thinking about other things to notice it at the time, but it was truly a beautiful song. It wasn't the lyrics that gave it beauty. But the way she sang it.. the way she was laughed and smiled as sang, the way she looked at us. The way my mother was watching her.

I used to say it was such a pity that we didn't record it. Although we didn't have access to any recording devices in those days. But the song is still fresh in my memory.. I even recall some of the lines.

Beetle beetle, little beetle Won't you speeeak?!! Won't you speak to meeee?!!

She kept on singing till she was tired and my aunt made her go to bed. She didn't want to, but obeyed her mother. She fell asleep quite was slept. She was indeed tired. But she was happy. She slept with a smile on her face.

I did switch on the computer afterwards, with Na. But I didn't feel like playing games. I gave the computer to Na. She played a little, but she didn't seem to enjoy it. Maybe Na realized what was bothering me earlier then. I never asked. She was a sensible girl even at that tender age. She understands people.

But I am sure the thought that I was annoyed at losing time with computer never crossed Ta's mind. She was happy, very happy, as she was singing.. Her song touched everyone who heard her. She was radiating happiness. Now, I think of Ta's song. I am happy. For a moment, I forget all my woes.. all my worries.. and am at peace..

I think Ta could sing such a song only because she was a child. A sweet innocent child not yet corrupt by affectations that adults often employ. Of course, Ta grew up. I doubt she would ever sing as she did back then when she a child again.

I didn't realize the value of that time when it was still not past. But I do now..

Ta's song is timeless.. A memory.. A sweet memory.. One that I would always cherish.. Her song is a token of her childhood.. And mine..
