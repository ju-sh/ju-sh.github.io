From Wikipedia:

If the syllable begins with a vowel sound, then the consonant ㅇ (ng) will act as a silent placeholder.

Shapes

Some of the consonents are based on the shape of tongue when pronouncing the sound.

  • ㅅ: s (or 'sh' rather??)
  • ㄴ: n (tongue when facing left)
  • ㄱ: g (tongue when facing left)
  • ㅁ: m (closed mouth)
  • ㅇ: ng (open mouth)

Usually three symbols per character??

안녕하세요 (annyeonghaseyo)

  • 안: ㅏ(a) + ㄴ(n)
  • 녕: ㄴ(n) + ㅕ (yeo) + ㅇ(ng)
  • 하: ㅎ(h) + ㅏ(a)
  • 세: ㅅ(s) + ㅔ(e)
  • 요: ㅛ(o)

한글 (hangul)

  • 한: ㅎ(h) + ㅏ(a) + ㄴ(n)
  • 글: ㄱ(g) + ㅡ(eu) + ㄹ(l)
