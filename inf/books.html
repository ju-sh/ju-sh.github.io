Focus on one thing at a time. Finish the things you start.

Look at how Franklin went about attaining his 13 goals to attain perfection in morality. He went about it by focusing on one goal per week.

like him who, having a garden to weed, does not attempt to eradicate all the bad herbs at once, which would exceed his reach and his strength, but works on one of the beds at a time, and, having accomplish'd the first, proceeds to a second, so I should have, I hoped, the encouraging pleasure of seeing [..] the progress I made

"Is it possible, when he is so great a writer? for I see him constantly at his escritoire."

"Yes," says Innis, "but he is like St. George on the signs, always on horseback, and never rides on."

This observation of the messenger was, it seems, well founded; for, when in England, I understood that Mr. Pitt gave it as one reason for removing this general, and sending Generals Amherst and Wolfe, that the minister never heard from him, and could not know what he was doing.
