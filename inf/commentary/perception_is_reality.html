Perception is reality

  • Date created: Jul-2021

You are whom you think you are. Whom you believe yourself to be.

As far as a person is concerned, things are the way that he thinks they are.

As Shakespeare said, ‘Beauty is in the eye of the beholder’.

Different people may define beauty in a different way.

No matter how messed up others may judge your life to be, if you think it’s not so bad, then it isn’t so bad.

Motivational speakers sometimes advice people to tell themselves ‘I can do it’ over and over again.

Why?

To make you believe it. So that you will be confident. So that you will see things differently. So that your perception of the world is changed.

Similarly, if you consider yourselves to be doomed, you are doomed.

Being able to live with oneself.

No matter how talented or capable a person is, if his confidence in himself is lost, and he thinks himself to be a failure, he is a failure.

It’s all a matter of perspective.

It is said that before its use (for which it is now well known) was discovered, people considered land which oozed petroleum to be useless.
What was considered worthless back then is now something much sought after (even to the extent of producing many violent conflicts).

Another story:
A man once gave his son a watch and asked him to find how much the watchmaker would give him for it.
The son reported that watchmaker, considering its derelict state, offered only a meagre sum.
The father nodded and asked him to see what the musuem curator says. The museum people identified it as an invaluable antique and offered a huge sum for it.

What one person values, another may not.

Find those who would value and enhance you and avoid the other category.

Remember that every coin has a flip side.

If there’s pain, there’s also joy. And vice-versa.

Of course, it’s still not always easy to bring yourself to believe in yourselves.

That’s the importance of good friends.

A safety social net of some sort. People who might be there to lend a helping hand if you go off course.
