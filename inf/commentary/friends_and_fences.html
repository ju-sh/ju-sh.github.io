Friendship and fences

  • Date created: Jul-2021

As Zhuge Liang said:

“The acquaintance of honourable people, even at a distance, does not add flowers in times of warmth and does not change its leaves in times of cold: it continues unfading through the four seasons, becomes increasingly stable as it passes through ease and danger.”

Strength and loyalty of such a friendship isn’t affected by your changing fortunes.

At least in some ways, such a friend doesn’t expect anything in return for their help.

Such a friend would stand by you the same in times of prosperity and adversity alike.

And as time goes on, the bond grows stronger. Increasingly stable, as Zhuge Liang put it.

A faithful friend is a sure shelter. He who has found one, has found a rare treasure.

How does a stable friendship begin?

It is not planned. It just happens.

Because if was planned, the acquaintance was made for a reason.

You have three types of friends in life: Friends for a reason, friends for a season, and friends for a lifetime. ¹

Aim for the last one. The other two are temporary and hence less likely to last long.

Friends for a reason: most common form of companionship. Formed with a particular aim in mind. Dissolved as soon as the aim is fulfilled or it becomes evident that it cannot come to fruition.

Friends for a season: quite common in workplaces. Can seem like an acquaintance that would last long, but usually doesn’t.

A great friend is someone who can stand your imperfections and whose imperfections you can stand.

Never explain yourselves. Your friends don’t need it and your enemies won’t believe you anyway.

The world’s yet to see a perfect human. (And is unlikely to see too, considering that ‘perfect’ is defined differently by different groups of people).

The only people worthy to be in your life are the ones that help you through the hard times and laugh with you after the hard times pass. ²

Remember, everything has a limit.

Love your neighbour, but don’t pull down the fence.

It’s won’t do to be distant mentally when near physically.

But it won’t do good to be too close either. Everything in excess amount is harmful, and human relations are no exception.

A middle ground would be best.. That’s a healthy relationship.

There’s this Malayalam proverb:

ചങ്ങാതി നന്നായാല്‍ കണ്ണാടി വേണ്ട.

English translation would be like ‘you don’t need a mirror if you have a good friend’.
