Tips

  • Integrity

  • It's better to be a bit straightforward instead of beating around the bush.

  • If you didn't understand something that's being discussed, don't allow yourself to pretend to have understood it to wiggle out of the situation.

  • Always make a record of the things that you have read and done.

    • Don't just read stuff, implement what you've read.
    • Helps you know if you really understood it.
    • 'Thinking about new ideas is usually more fun than writing up old ones'[1]. Don't fall into this trap.

Research work

  • How would you compare your result with pre-existing work?
    • Are there well-known benchmarks?
    • You need stats
  • What real life problem does your work aim to solve?
    • Know the answer to this before embarking on the quest to find a solution.
  • Your advisor is just the guide. You should be the one in the driving seat. Advisor, who's more experienced, can spot potential problems and tell you so that you catch turn the steering wheel appropriately.
  • Break up the big task into simple, small, achievable, time-bound aims.
    • S: Specific
    • M: Measurable
    • A: Acheivable
    • R: Relevant
    • T: Time-bound

  • You'll never finish every project you start.
    • Drop the less promising ones and focus on the better ones. Otherwise you won't have time.

  • What's best for you may not always be what's best for your advisor. A conflict of interest is usual.

  • By the time you finish PhD, you should transform from a follower to someone other people follow.
